Dr. Nkrumah Mausoleum

The mausoleum was constructed as a tribute to Dr. Nkrumah's pivotal role in leading Ghana to become the first African nation to gain independence in 1957.
His vision extended beyond Ghana,as he was a strong advocate for Pan-African unity and worked tirelessly to support the decolonization of other African nations.
Despite being ousted from power in a coup in 1966 and living in exile until his death in 1972,
Nkrumah's contributions to Ghana and Africa's liberation were later widely recognized and celebrated.
